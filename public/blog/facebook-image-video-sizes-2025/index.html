Facebook is still the world's largest social platform with over 3 billion monthly users. It also has the most complex set of image and video specs — different rules apply to profiles, pages, groups, events, ads, and stories. This guide covers everything in one place.

Facebook Post Image Sizes

Facebook scales images in the feed based on their original ratio. Landscape images appear wider; square images appear as squares. Facebook renders images at up to 2048px wide on high-density screens, so uploading at 1200px or above is recommended.

Image TypeRecommended SizeAspect RatioNotes
Shared / Feed Image1200×630 px1.91:1Most common format; sRGB color space
Square Post1080×1080 px1:1
Portrait Post1080×1350 px4:5Max portrait ratio allowed
Photo Album Cover1200×630 px1.91:1First photo in the album
Shared Link Preview1200×630 px1.91:1Pulled from Open Graph meta tags
Compression tipFacebook recompresses JPEGs significantly. For the sharpest results, upload as PNG (for graphics with text) or upload high-quality JPEGs at 85%+ quality with sRGB color profile.

Facebook Ad Image Specs

Facebook ads share the same underlying image specs as organic posts, but ads have stricter requirements around text overlay. Facebook's ad system historically penalized images with more than 20% text — that rule has been relaxed, but high-text images still see reduced delivery.

Ad TypeRecommended SizeAspect RatioMax File Size
Single Image Ad1200×628 px1.91:130 MB
Single Image (square)1080×1080 px1:130 MB
Carousel Card1080×1080 px1:130 MB per card
Stories Ad1080×1920 px9:1630 MB
Reels Ad1080×1920 px9:164 GB video
Right Column Ad1200×628 px1.91:130 MB
Ad tipFor Facebook and Instagram feed ads using the same creative, design at 1080×1350 px (4:5). It takes up the most feed space on both platforms without being cropped, and will be automatically rendered as 1.91:1 in right-column placements.
